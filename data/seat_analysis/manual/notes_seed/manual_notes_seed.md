ltanpur

What happened:
Sultanpur should be analysed as a competitive Uttar Pradesh seat where candidate profile and alliance arithmetic matter.

Why it mattered:
It is useful for understanding whether the BJP's UP losses were isolated or part of a wider shift in eastern/central UP.

Factors that may have mattered:
Caste mobilisation, local candidate reputation, SP-Congress coordination, and turnout.

What to watch:
Whether BJP recovers through candidate replacement/organisation or whether the opposition consolidates further.

Kairana

What happened:
Kairana is an important western UP seat with a history of polarisation and alliance politics.

Why it mattered:
It tests whether BJP polarisation politics remains dominant in western UP or whether caste-community alliances can counter it.

Factors that may have mattered:
Jat-Muslim dynamics, SP/RLD/BJP alliance shifts, candidate selection, and local communal history.

What to watch:
Whether the BJP's western UP coalition stabilises or opposition/regional alignments reopen the seat.

Jadavpur

What happened:
Jadavpur remained important as a politically expressive Kolkata-area seat.

Why it mattered:
The seat is useful for understanding urban Bengal politics, where ideology, students, middle-class voters, and TMC organisational strength all interact.

Factors that may have mattered:
TMC's urban organisation, anti-BJP consolidation, Left/Congress weakness or fragmentation, and local candidate appeal.

What to watch:
Whether BJP can grow in Kolkata-adjacent seats or remains blocked by TMC's urban machine.

Tamluk

What happened:
Tamluk is a high-interest Bengal seat because of its history with anti-Left politics and later BJP/TMC competition.

Why it mattered:
It helps show whether BJP's Bengal growth is concentrated in specific sub-regions rather than uniform across the state.

Factors that may have mattered:
Local organisational strength, candidate profile, Medinipur-region political networks, and TMC-BJP polarisation.

What to watch:
Whether Tamluk becomes a durable BJP foothold or swings with Bengal's broader state mood.

Dum Dum

What happened:
Dum Dum is a dense urban/suburban Bengal seat where TMC strength has been difficult for BJP to break.

Why it mattered:
It captures the challenge BJP faces in Kolkata-adjacent constituencies despite statewide vote-share ambitions.

Factors that may have mattered:
Urban working-class networks, TMC organisation, minority/local consolidation, and opposition fragmentation.

What to watch:
Whether BJP's Bengal ceiling is lower in metropolitan seats than in northern or western Bengal.

Chhindwara

What happened:
Chhindwara is one of the most important Congress legacy seats in Madhya Pradesh.

Why it mattered:
It has long been associated with Kamal Nath's political base. Any BJP breakthrough here would represent not just a seat gain but the weakening of a Congress regional fortress.

Factors that may have mattered:
Local networks, candidate inheritance, BJP's statewide dominance, and Congress organisational erosion.

What to watch:
Whether Congress can retain regional strongholds in BJP-dominant states, or whether even legacy seats become vulnerable.

Mandi

What happened:
Mandi became a high-profile Himachal seat because of candidate visibility and BJP's strength in the state.

Why it mattered:
It is a seat where celebrity, party organisation, and state-level political mood intersect.

Factors that may have mattered:
Candidate recognition, BJP's Himachal organisation, local identity, and the Congress state government's performance.

What to watch:
Whether Mandi behaves like a BJP-safe seat or becomes more competitive under state anti-incumbency.

Kolkata Dakshin

What happened:
Kolkata Dakshin remains a key TMC urban seat.

Why it mattered:
It reflects the TMC's hold over Kolkata's political imagination and the difficulty BJP faces in breaking into the city's core constituencies.

Factors that may have mattered:
Urban Bengali identity, TMC welfare networks, candidate familiarity, and anti-BJP consolidation.

What to watch:
Whether BJP's Bengal strategy can penetrate Kolkata proper, or whether its future growth remains outside the city core.
